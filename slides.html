The Data Behind the Story

Five public datasets — cross-referenced to build a single picture of displacement.

🏠

MAPC Residential Sales

Every residential property sale in Boston 2000–2023 — who bought, how much, and whether the buyer is an investor entity.

~120,000 transactions
⚖️

MA Trial Court Evictions

All eviction filings 2020–2024 — case type, plaintiff, outcome. Geocoded to address for mapping.

6,484 Boston cases
📊

Boston Assessment Rolls

Property-level ownership, unit counts, and assessed values from the city — used to identify corporate vs. individual landlords.

Per-parcel, annual
📈

Zillow ZORI

Zillow's Observed Rent Index — monthly median rent by neighborhood, 2016–2025. Used to show rent trajectory over time.

Neighborhood-level
🏙️

BPDA + Census

Neighborhood boundaries, renter median household income, and demographic composition from the American Community Survey.

ACS 5-year estimates
🔗

How We Combined Them

Sales data → ownership classification. Eviction records → geocoded and joined to ownership. ZORI → matched to BPDA neighborhoods.

Custom pipeline

What's Left
in Roxbury?

74
units priced out at a $2,000/mo budget
since 2019 — gone from the market.

In 2019, a $2,000/mo budget reached 121 rentals in Roxbury. Today it reaches only 47. 74 units have effectively vanished for working-class renters — not demolished, just unaffordable.

In our visualization, those 74 dots fall off the map.
